# RH20T data — per-cfg analysis

Everything below is regenerated by `preprocessing/analyze_cfgs.py` (add `--full-scan`
for the missing-file sweep). Numbers verified 2026-07-04 against the unpacked data.

## Layout on disk

```
/mnt/nas/data/RH20T/            (=$RH20T)
  raw/          RH20T_cfg1 … RH20T_cfg7   untarred scenes; calib+joint patch merged into cfg1/2
  frames/       cfg1/ … cfg7/             extract_frames output (scene/cam_*/color/<ts>.jpg)
  shards/       cfg1/ … cfg7/             make_shards output (WebDataset .tar + count.txt)
  checkpoints/                            training outputs
```

Each raw scene: `cam_<serial>/color.mp4 + timestamps.npy`, `transformed/*.npy`
(robot state, dicts keyed by camera serial), `audio_mixed/*.wav` (not always present).

## The 7 configs are 4 different robots

| cfg | robot | DOF | gripper | F/T sensor | joint.npy vec len | contents |
|-----|-------|-----|---------|------------|-------------------|----------|
| 1 | Flexiv Rizon | 7 | Dahuan AG-95 | dahuan | **14** | 7 pos + 7 vel |
| 2 | Flexiv Rizon | 7 | Dahuan AG-95 | dahuan | **14** | 7 pos + 7 vel |
| 3 | UR5 | 6 | WSG-50 | ati | **6** | 6 pos |
| 4 | UR5 | 6 | Robotiq 2F-85 | ati | **6** | 6 pos |
| 5 | Franka | 7 | franka | **none** | **14** | 7 pos + 7 vel |
| 6 | KUKA iiwa | 7 | Robotiq 2F-85 | ati | **21** | 7 pos + 7 vel + 7 torque |
| 7 | KUKA iiwa | 7 | Robotiq 2F-85 | ati | **21** | 7 pos + 7 vel + 7 torque |

**The joint.npy vector is NOT "N joint angles"** — it concatenates position with
velocity (and torque on KUKA). Joint *positions* are always the first `DOF` values
(`robot_joint_field` in rh20t_api's `configs.json`). Anything that consumes joints must
slice by DOF, not take the whole vector.

Consistent across all cfgs (verified per scene):
- `tcp_base.npy`: per-ts dicts, `tcp` is always **(7,)** pos+quat; `robot_ft` (6,).
- `force_torque_base.npy`: per-ts dicts, `zeroed`/`raw` always **(6,)**. Present even
  for cfg5 (Franka, no external sensor) — those values are robot-internal estimates,
  treat with caution.
- `gripper.npy`: `{serial: {ts: {gripper_command: [width, …], gripper_info: […]}}}` —
  `gripper_command` present everywhere. Gripper *hardware* differs (3 models), so raw
  widths are not directly comparable across cfgs.
- Camera count per scene: 7-10 (varies within a cfg, e.g. cfg4 has 7 and 8).
- cfg7 additionally has `transformed/tactile.npy` (unique to it).
- `audio_mixed/`: present in all cfgs but **not every scene** (some cfg1/2 scenes have
  0 wavs; some have 2+ segments).

## Anomalies / traps

- **`_human` vs `_human_2` naming**: human demos come in `…_human` AND `…_human_2`
  variants. `extract_frames._is_scene` filters with `endswith("_human")`, so the 543
  `_human_2` scenes **leaked into frames/ and the stage-1 shards** (frames dirs ≈ robot
  + `_human_2`). Filter with `"_human" in name` instead. Impact: ~4% extra (robot-less)
  video in stage-1 pretraining — harmless for video-only JEPA, but stage-2 state
  loading must exclude them (they have no robot state).
- **57 robot scenes (~0.4%) lack `transformed/joint.npy`** and must be skipped:
  cfg1: 3, cfg2: 4, cfg3: 1, cfg4: 17, cfg5: 4, cfg6: 8, cfg7: 20.
  Full list: `preprocessing/analyze_cfgs.py --full-scan`.
- A few scenes have cameras without `color.mp4` (extract_frames already skips them);
  frames dir counts are within a handful of the expected scene counts (cfg1 −1,
  cfg2 −1, cfg4 −6).
- cfg5 F/T is not from a physical sensor (see above).

## Implications for stage-2 (mm_perceiver) training beyond cfg3/4

The current state pipeline hardcodes cfg3's UR5 shape and breaks on 5 of 7 cfgs:

- `world_tokenizer/state.py`: `SceneState.state()` sin/cos's the **whole** joint vector
  → 28 dims only when it's 6 long; cfg1/2/5 give 44, cfg6/7 give 58. `STATE_DIM=28`
  and `FT_DIMS=range(21,27)` are absolute offsets that shift with any joint change.
- `world_tokenizer/step1_gate.py` `GROUPS` slices assume the same 28-dim layout.
- `world_tokenizer/precompute_patch.py` hardcodes `RAW=…/raw/RH20T_cfg3` independent of
  `--frames-root` (pointing frames at another cfg would silently pair its images with
  cfg3 state) and would cache whatever dim `state()` returns, unchecked.
- `world_tokenizer/train_perceiver.py` constructs `MMPerceiver()` without `state_dim`,
  so the model expects 28 regardless of the cache.

Open design decisions before the full run (not yet implemented):
1. **Joint unification** — slice positions per DOF and pad 6-DOF robots to 7
   (STATE_DIM 28→30), vs also keeping velocities (zeros for UR5), vs truncating to 6.
2. **Robot identity** — purely physical state vs appending a cfg/robot one-hot.
3. **Cache layout** — one npz per cfg on the NAS (resumable, concat at train time)
   vs a single combined cache (~58 GB at 15 frames/scene, fp16).
4. **Frame budget** — 15 frames/scene ≈ 192k samples (matches the cfg3 POC density).
